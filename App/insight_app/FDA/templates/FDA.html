The popularity of health applications (e.g. WebMD) has inspired the public to seek and gather more information on their health and take more initiative to control it. Despite intentions this sometimes does more harm than good. Their insistance on transparency leads to health apps presenting very alarming diagnoses for mundane characteristics. One minute, you're complaining of a headache and the next you're stressing about brain aneurysms.

The situation gets worse when difficult to pronounce chemicals are involved, namely prescription drugs. In this application I aim to assuage a concerned user's fears stemming from the use of new or unknown drugs. Assuming they have an adverse reaction from one of the drugs in their profile, I predict the likelihood of a serious outcome from the hypothetical adversity.
